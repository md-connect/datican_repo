Human health conditions have been a significant global concern. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), the mortality rate of Cardiovascular diseases is 17.9 Million per year, over 1 billion people are hypertensive, about 422 million people have diabetes and Breast Cancer is predicted to be 3 Million per year by 2050.

Majority of these diseases are prevalent in low and middle-income countries like Nigeria. Manual diagnosis of diseases could lead to wrong diagnosis, delayed diagnosis and also subjectivity.

Data Science Potential

Data science has been demonstrated to be useful in early diagnosis of these conditions in the developed world but Africa is lagging. The continent faces unique challenges that limit the adoption of data-driven healthcare solutions.

The Challenge

The goal of DATICAN is to attempt to solve these problems. We're building a bridge between medical expertise and data science in Nigeria.

Challenges We're Addressing

Shortage of Medical Personnel

Lack of medical personnel interested in data science

Shortage of Computer Scientists

Lack of computer scientists interested in medicine

Lack of Computational Resources

Limited access to computing infrastructure for research

Lack of Accessible Medical Data

Shortage of curated, accessible medical datasets

The Goal of DATICAN

To build a bridge between medical expertise and data science in Nigeria by creating curated local datasets, fostering interdisciplinary collaboration, providing computational resources, and training the next generation of medical data scientists.
